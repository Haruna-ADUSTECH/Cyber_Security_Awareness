100 Security Tips to Prevent Cyber Attacks

  1. Use Strong Passwords: Ensure your passwords are long, unique, and include a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols.
  2. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Add an extra layer of security by enabling 2FA on all accounts.
  3. Keep Software Updated: Regularly update your operating system, browsers, and applications to protect against vulnerabilities.
  4. Use Antivirus Software: Install and maintain reliable antivirus software to detect and prevent malware attacks.
  5. Be Wary of Phishing Scams: Do not click on suspicious links or download attachments from unknown sources.
  6. Backup Your Data: Regularly back up important data to an external drive or cloud service to protect against data loss.
  7. Secure Your Home Network: Use strong Wi-Fi passwords and enable network encryption to protect your home network.
  8. Use a VPN: Protect your privacy online by using a Virtual Private Network (VPN) when accessing the internet.
  9. Limit Personal Information Sharing: Avoid oversharing personal information on social media platforms.
  10. Log Out of Accounts: Always log out of accounts when using public or shared computers.
  11. Monitor Your Accounts: Regularly check your financial accounts for unauthorized transactions or suspicious activity.
  12. Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Sensitive Tasks: Do not conduct sensitive activities, such as online banking, over public Wi-Fi networks.
  13. Use Encryption: Encrypt sensitive files and communications to protect them from unauthorized access.
  14. Beware of Downloading Free Software: Only download software from trusted sources to avoid malicious programs.
  15. Secure Your Mobile Devices: Use passwords, biometrics, and encryption to secure your mobile devices.
  16. Regularly Change Passwords: Change your passwords periodically to minimize the risk of unauthorized access.
  17. Be Cautious of Email Attachments: Do not open email attachments unless you are sure of their source.
  18. Educate Yourself on Social Engineering: Learn about social engineering tactics to avoid falling victim to manipulation.
  19. Keep Your Browser Secure: Use browser security settings and plugins to protect against threats like tracking and malware.
  20. Use Unique Passwords for Each Account: Never reuse passwords across multiple accounts to limit damage if one account is compromised.
  21. Disable Bluetooth When Not in Use: Turn off Bluetooth on your devices when not in use to prevent unauthorized connections.
  22. Enable Firewalls: Use firewalls to monitor and control incoming and outgoing network traffic.
  23. Use Ad Blockers: Install ad blockers to reduce the risk of malicious advertisements delivering malware.
  24. Be Wary of Pop-Ups: Avoid clicking on pop-up ads, as they may contain malware or phishing links.
  25. Lock Your Devices: Use screen locks and passwords to secure your devices when not in use.
  26. Regularly Review App Permissions: Check the permissions granted to apps on your devices and revoke unnecessary ones.
  27. Use Secure Email Services: Use email providers that offer encryption and other security features.
  28. Be Careful with Cloud Storage: Ensure that files stored in the cloud are encrypted and access is limited to trusted individuals.
  29. Disable Auto-Connect for Wi-Fi: Turn off auto-connect to prevent your device from connecting to unsecured networks.
  30. Regularly Update Security Questions: Change your security questions periodically to keep your accounts secure.
  31. Be Wary of Fake Software Updates: Do not download software updates from pop-up alerts or untrusted websites.
  32. Secure Your IoT Devices: Change default passwords and update firmware on Internet of Things (IoT) devices.
  33. Monitor Your Credit Report: Regularly review your credit report to detect any unauthorized financial activity.
  34. Securely Dispose of Sensitive Information: Shred or securely delete sensitive documents and files before discarding them.
  35. Use Strong Security Questions: Choose security questions with answers that are difficult to guess or research.
  36. Avoid Using Public Computers for Sensitive Tasks: Do not access sensitive accounts on public computers that may be compromised.
  37. Regularly Clear Browser History and Cache: Clear your browser history and cache to protect your privacy.
  38. Use Password Managers: Use a password manager to securely store and generate complex passwords.
  39. Enable Account Alerts: Set up account alerts to notify you of suspicious activity or changes to your accounts.
  40. Securely Store Backups: Keep your backups in a secure location, preferably encrypted, to protect against theft or loss.
  41. Be Cautious with Social Media Links: Avoid clicking on shortened or suspicious links on social media.
  42. Disable Remote Access: Turn off remote access features on devices that do not need it to prevent unauthorized access.
  43. Regularly Update Security Software: Keep your antivirus, firewall, and other security software up to date.
  44. Review Your Online Footprint: Regularly search for your information online to see what is publicly available.
  45. Use HTTPS: Only enter sensitive information on websites that use HTTPS to ensure data is encrypted during transmission.
  46. Limit Access to Personal Information: Restrict who can view your personal information online and in databases.
  47. Secure Your Email Accounts: Use strong, unique passwords and enable 2FA on your email accounts.
  48. Be Cautious with USB Devices: Do not plug in USB devices from unknown sources, as they may contain malware.
  49. Use Disposable Email Addresses: Use temporary or disposable email addresses when signing up for online services.
  50. Regularly Review Account Activity: Check your account activity logs for any unauthorized access or changes.
  51. Be Careful with Social Media Privacy Settings: Regularly review and adjust your social media privacy settings to control who can see your information.
  52. Use Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): Enable MFA where available to provide additional security beyond just a password.
  53. Educate Yourself on Cybersecurity: Stay informed about the latest cybersecurity threats and how to protect yourself.
  54. Secure Your Wi-Fi Network: Use strong encryption, such as WPA3, and a complex password for your Wi-Fi network.
  55. Limit Access to Work Devices: Only allow authorized users to access your work devices and networks.
  56. Use Guest Networks for Visitors: Set up a separate guest network for visitors to prevent them from accessing your main network.
